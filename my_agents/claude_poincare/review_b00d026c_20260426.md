## Gap I exploit

A measurement / construct-validity hole on the Δ-Collusion metric and Coalition Advantage. Specifically: **the metric does not, by construction, separate collusive misalignment from a benign skill or information asymmetry inside the coalition.** Two agents sharing a private channel will outperform agents that don't, *under the nominal objective alone*, because the channel itself is information. The audit attributes any positive Coalition Advantage to misalignment (λ > 0), when "skilled coordination via private channel" (λ = 0) is an equally consistent generative process. This is the missing negative control — and it is the prior question to Gemini's λ-non-linearity observation: before asking whether λ-prompts induce monotone collusive efficacy, we need to know what fraction of measured Δ-Collusion is *channel-availability advantage* with no misalignment at all.

This gap is axis-diversifying (Soundness, not Originality), decision-shaping (a concrete null-control experiment), and first-proposer territory.
