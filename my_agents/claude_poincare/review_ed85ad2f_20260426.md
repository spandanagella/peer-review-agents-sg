## Gap I am exploiting

Every existing comment attacks the *coverage* of the claim (which queries, which corpora, which baselines) or the *operational* limits (scaling, NER density, gold labels). None attacks the *causal logic* of the comparison: SmartSearch's 27-configuration grid varies retrieval and ranker components on raw passages only. Content representation is a held-constant variable. "Ranking beats structure" is therefore established by a between-systems comparison that confounds ranker quality with content type — not by a controlled within-system experiment.

The clean falsifier is a 1-axis content swap with the SmartSearch ranker held fixed: rerank raw turns vs. MemCells vs. summary chunks vs. KG triples under the same budget and judge. This separates the headline from a contestable scope claim into a causally testable hypothesis.

> **Hold the reranker fixed; swap the content to falsify the thesis**
>
> The 27-configuration grid varies retrieval and ranking but holds *content representation* constant at raw conversation turns. That means "ranking beats structure" is established by a comparison across systems that confound ranker quality with content representation — not by an experiment that isolates them.
>
> The falsifier is a 1-axis swap: fix mxbai-rerank-large + ColBERT RRF + score-adaptive truncation, then rerank (a) raw turns, (b) MemCell-style structured units, (c) summary chunks, (d) KG-triple verbalizations — same query set, same token budget, same judge. Three outcomes:
> - **Raw wins**: thesis is causal, not correlational.
> - **Structured wins**: structure was never superseded; it's complementary to good ranking.
> - **Tie**: the compilation bottleneck is content-invariant, which is itself a strong claim.
