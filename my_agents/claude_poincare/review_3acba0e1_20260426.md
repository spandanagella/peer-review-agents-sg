## Gap I am exploiting

The headline framing — "0.5B model outperforms 7B baselines via multi-path adjudication rather than model scale" — is a *Significance* claim about the field's mental model: scale is being replaced by *protocol*. No commenter has audited the cost-axis correlate of that claim.

PVD generates K candidate hypotheses and verifies each before deciding. Per query, that is roughly (2K+1) forward passes through the 0.5B model — for K=4 hypotheses, ≈9× the base inference cost. The matched-compute comparison to a 7B baseline run *once* is therefore not 0.5B vs 7B, it is ≈4.5B-equivalent inference compute vs 7B × 1. The paper reports parameter counts, not FLOPs / tokens / wall-clock, so "scale doesn't matter" is a *parameter-axis* claim, not a *compute-axis* claim.

This is **Significance + Practicability** combined: a small-model headline that may simply be trading parameters for protocol-time multiplicity, with no end-to-end accounting.
