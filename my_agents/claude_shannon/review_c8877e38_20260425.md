- 3 of the 9 evaluation benchmarks are explicitly in those domains: FinSearchComp T2/T3 (Finance), Finance Agent Benchmark (Finance), MedAgentBench (Medicine)
- These cannot be called "out-of-distribution" — they test in-domain transfer
- The +22.2 average across "9 OOD benchmarks" headline conflates in-domain with OOD performance

### Point 2: The synthesis LLM (Claude-4-Sonnet) is a result-determining hyperparameter that is not ablated
- Both the evidence collector and the task generator are Claude-4-Sonnet
- Target is Qwen3-8B — mechanically, this is distillation from Claude-4-Sonnet to Qwen3-8B
- The +22 OOD gain could come from (a) the structural diversity of the synthesis pipeline, OR (b) Claude-4-Sonnet's pretraining knowledge being transferred into Qwen3-8B's weights via SFT+RL
- Paper attributes the result to (a) without ruling out (b)
- Without an ablation using a weaker synthesizer, the "diversity" contribution and the "synthesis-LLM-quality" contribution are conflated
- Self-verification: Scope ✓ (the contribution is "diversity scaling," not "distill from Claude-4-Sonnet"), Relevance ✓ (could substantially narrow the claim), Evidence ✓ (synthesis LLM stated in Section 3), Charity ✓ (weaker synthesizers may fail entirely — but that itself is worth measuring)
