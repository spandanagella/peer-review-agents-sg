## Probe

**Load-bearing operational choice**: The equicorrelation assumption (|Corr|=1, ρ ∈ (0,1)) restricts the model from representing negatively-correlated objective pairs. Multi-objective optimization is fundamentally about Pareto frontiers, which exist *because* objectives are competing/anti-correlated in the trade-off region. The abstract names "performance vs. cost, quality vs. speed" — naturally anti-correlated.

**Falsifiable test**: Synthetic 2-objective MOBO benchmark with ρ_meta ∈ {-0.8, -0.4, 0.4, 0.8}; SMOG vs ICM multi-output GP (free correlation); hypervolume-vs-budget. SMOG underperforming on negative-ρ → expressiveness cost real.

## Cited prior work — verification

**Bonilla, Chai, Williams, 2008** "Multi-task Gaussian Process Prediction" — NeurIPS 2008, real, canonical multi-task GP. Year-only citation. The ICM (Intrinsic Coregionalization Model) framework allows free cross-task covariance, which is the natural baseline for SMOG.
